## Markets and Things Happening

### 1. Oranjezicht Market - also known as Granger Bay Market
Food stalls, coffee, fresh fruit & veg, plants, other items. Great vibe.
>Venue: V&A Waterfront
>Open: Sat & Sun, 9am-1pm, Wed 4-8pm (Nov - March)
>[More info](https://ozcf.co.za/market-day/)


### 2. Old Biscuit Mill
Always packed, nice morning out. Food stalls, coffee, other bits and pieces.
Rosetta makes good coffee near the Biscuit Mill (in Woodstock Exchange)
>Venue: Old Biscuit Mill, Albert Road, Woodstock
>Open: Sat 9am-3pm
>[More info](http://www.neighbourgoodsmarket.co.za/visitor-info)

### 3. First Thursdays
First Thursday of every month - Along Bree St and in town - night market and all
shops and bars open late etc.
Check out the Hidden Gin Bar behind _Honest Chocolate_ in Wale St
>Venue: Bree St, Long St, around there
>Open: First Thursday of every month, 6-10pm
>[More info](https://first-thursdays.co.za/cape-town/)

### 4. Zeitz MOCAA
Contempory Art museum at Silo Hotel
>Venue: Silo Hotel, V&A Waterfront
>Open: most days
>[More info](https://zeitzmocaa.museum/)

## Must Do

Book sundowner drinks on the rooftop at the Silo Hotel at the waterfront. You
have to reserve a place and they only allow a certain amount of people up every
day. The drink may be a little pricey but the view and experience is well worth
it

Kayak at sunrise - there’s a place in Mouille Point that does this - [Atlantic Outlook](https://www.atlanticoutlook.com/)

## Restaurants

El Burro - Greenpoint: Mexican with a fun vibe - their bar downstairs also gets
going on the weekend

Charango - Peruvian and always full - Bree St, CBD. More for the people watching
and vibe than the food

Kloof St House - Open late night, great bar - upmarket but some interesting
local dishes. Won’t disappoint

Bocca - Bree St, CBD, Pizza and tapas - I’ve always had good meals here. No
reservations

Thali - fancy Indian - amazing - Gardens

Hudson’s Burgers - Greenpoint - Burgers

Rick’s American - Gardens - Burgers

Black Sheep - Kloof St

The commissary - fancy street food

## Breakfast / Brunch / Lunch

Jarryd’s - Sea Point - everything is good. Seriously

Between Us - top of Bree St - my absolute favorite at the moment. Muddled eggs
are amazing

Clarkes - Bree st, kinda grungy cool place for all day breakfast. Very popular

Strangers Club - Greenpoint - my secret breakfast and coffee spot - quiet, great
to work. I love the place

Hemelhuijs - 71 Waterkant St - CBD

Kleinsky’s - bagels and breakfast - Sea Point

Scheckter’s Raw - Sea Point - vegan and vegetarian and raw food delights.
Amazingly popular. Trump Dog hot dog is great. Raw snickers bar good too.

Jason's on Bree St or Main Road Greenpoint - pastries and breakfast

## Best Beaches

Bakoven (left of Camps Bay - small cute cove like beach)

Beta beach - close to bakhoven. A locals spot

Glen Beach ( between camps bay and Clifton 4th) - great to watch surfers

Clifton 1 or Clifton 2

Llandudno - stick to the corners

Boulders Beach - go visit the penguins

Cape Point- national park at the tip of the peninsula. Ride the furnicular.

Camps Bay tidal pool great as the sun is rising for a quick swim

St James - Multi colored huts lining a small kids beach. Worth a visit. In fact
go to Kalk Bay for the day, have ice cream and walk to St James along the coast
and the train tracks

Amazing picnic spot and beach hideout - Oudekraal ( has an entrance fee) - just
beyond the 12 apostles hotel

Botanical Gardens - Kirstenbosch

## Hikes
